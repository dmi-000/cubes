Notes on orbit-size structure (probed empirically, not hand-derived):
- A GENERIC full quaternion has orbit size 3 (C3), 2 (C2), 6 (C6), 4 (D2) --
  so C3:3+3, C2:2+2+2, C6:6 and D2:4+free2 accept full-quaternion seeds for
  every block (core AND free). This is where the full-quat expansion pays off
  (C3 399->705, D2 651->717, C2 653->677).
- A generic full quaternion has orbit size 12 (T) and 6 (D3); the size-4 (T)
  and size-3 (D3) orbits that T:4+free2 / D3:3+3 need require the seed to lie
  on an alignment locus (a C3- resp. C2-stabilized coset) -- a genuine
  1-parameter angle family, not reachable by a uniform quaternion draw. Those
  cores are therefore gridded over their aligned angle (their true DOF) with
  full-quaternion FREE cubes; they reproduce their run-1 floors (661, 657),
  confirming those were already the family maxima on the alignment locus.
